About this Tree

Unlike the common green or yellow coconut, the King Coconut is a distinct cultivar known for its bright orange husk and naturally sweet, low-fat water. It is grown mainly for drinking rather than for copra or oil, and is considered a specialty variety in India.

Did You Know?

  • The orange colour comes from natural pigments in the husk, not ripeness.
  • King Coconut water has slightly more sugar and electrolytes than regular coconut water.
  • A single healthy palm can produce 60-100 fruits a year.
  • Coconut palms can live and bear fruit for over 60 years.

Uses & Benefits

  • Fresh drinking water (tender fruit)
  • Husk fibre for coir products
  • Leaves for thatching and weaving
  • Shell for handicrafts and fuel
