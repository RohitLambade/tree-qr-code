About this Tree

The Ashoka tree holds deep cultural and religious significance in India and is increasingly rare in the wild, even though it is commonly planted.

Did You Know?

  • Flowers open yellow and turn orange-red as they age
  • Mentioned in classical Indian literature

Uses & Benefits

  • Ornamental planting
  • Bark used in traditional medicine
