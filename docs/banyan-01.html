About this Tree

The Banyan is famous for its aerial prop roots, which grow down from its branches and thicken into new trunks over time, allowing a single tree to spread across a huge area. It is considered sacred in Indian culture and is a symbol of longevity and shelter.

Did You Know?

  • The Great Banyan in Kolkata's botanical garden covers over 14,000 square metres.
  • Banyan trees are 'strangler figs' - they often start life growing on another host tree.
  • The tree can live for hundreds of years.
  • Its dense canopy supports birds, insects, and small mammals as habitat.

Uses & Benefits

  • Shade and shelter (community gathering point)
  • Habitat for birds and insects
  • Bark and leaves used in traditional medicine
  • Cultural and religious significance
